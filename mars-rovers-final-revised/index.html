The Mars Exploration Project

For years, NASA’s Mars Exploration Program has been peeling back the layers of the Red Planet, uncovering a world far more complex than the dusty sphere we see in the night sky. Mars holds the remnants of ancient rivers, the chemistry of long-lost lakes, and geological clues that hint at a time when the planet may have been warm, wet, and possibly even habitable. Each mission sent to its surface brings us closer to understanding how this neighboring world fits into the grand story of our solar system.

To unlock these mysteries, NASA relies on a remarkable fleet of spacecraft working together like a scientific relay team. Orbiters scan the planet from above, mapping canyons, craters, and dried riverbeds in extraordinary detail. Landers study the soil and weather right where they touch down. And the rovers—NASA’s most adventurous explorers—traverse ancient landscapes, drilling into rocks, analyzing minerals, and searching for the chemical fingerprints of past life. Every mission builds on the last, revealing a Mars that was once far more dynamic than we imagined.

At the center of this exploration are Curiosity and Perseverance, two car-sized robotic scientists roaming the surface today. Curiosity digs into the planet’s past habitability, while Perseverance hunts for signs of ancient life and collects samples for a future return to Earth. Their discoveries continue to reshape what we know about Mars—and the best part is, their journey is still unfolding.
